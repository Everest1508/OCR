FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

OCR stands for "Optical Character Recognition." It is a technology that recognizes text within a digital image. It is commonly used to recognize text in scanned documents and images. OCR software can be used to convert a physical paper document, or an image into an accessible electronic version with text.

Step 1 : Click on Browse Image.
Step 2 : Upload image in our System
Step 3 : Click on Image to text Button
Step 4 : Output will be shown in Textbox

Any image with text is supported,example JPG/JPEG, PNG, BMP etc.Image resolution should be 200 DPI or higher to ensure good recognition results.

OCR can be used for a variety of applications, including the following: Scanning printed documents into versions that can be edited with word processors, like Microsoft Word or Google Docs. Indexing print material for search engines. Automating data entry, extraction and processing.

OCR technology provides a much more sophisticated output compared to scanning as instead of capturing the document as an image, it identifies the characters and converts them into machine-readable text. This process allows you to edit the text, search for keywords, and retrieve information faster.

Step 1 : Click on input box saying "Paste your text here".
Step 2 : Paste your text.
Step 3 : Click on Text to Image Button
Step 4 : Image will be downloaded soon

A simple OCR engine works by storing many different font and text image patterns as templates. The OCR software uses pattern-matching algorithms to compare text images, character by character, to its internal database. Our system uses LSTM Model to extract the text.
